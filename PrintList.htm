How to Read Your List

This list of colleges was created to closely match the interests and qualifications you reported on the questionnaire. The information contained in this list is provided for informational purposes and only should be used as a starting point in determining the schools to which you should apply. The list is neither a guarantee of acceptance nor of rejection. Because the college selection process is of such importance, you should research to determine a final list of schools.

The (+) or (-) next to the school indicates that the school is either more (+) or less (-) competitive than other schools in the same category.

GPA Calculation

Please note that the grade point average used to generate this list of schools was based on your unweighted average in core academic subjects (English, math, science, social studies, and foreign language). In addition, we have carefully evaluated the rigor of the curriculum on your transcript and factored that information into the placement of schools on your list. Colleges will typically evaluate both your unweighted average in these subjects and the rigor of your curriculum when reviewing your application for admission.

Special Notes About Sophia's List

Your college list was created using the following criteria – Unweighted GPA in an AP/college prep curriculum: A-; SAT: 1450 (670 R, 780 M); Major – business. A preference was given to medium-sized, urban schools when possible. Please note that the placements on this list are for general admission to the college or university only. Next Steps


1. Begin Your Research

You've received your suggested list of schools. Now what? The schools on your list are a good beginning, but the list is not an exhaustive list of every school that might interest you. Instead, the list is a starting point, a working document to which you can add or subtract schools based on your research. But your main focus now becomes winnowing down that list of schools to a more manageable number of schools to visit.


2. Find Your Best Fit

At College Coach we are big believers in 'fit.' Choosing the right college is much more than just considering majors, activities and a good location. It's about understanding the importance of fit.


3. Plan Your Campus Visits

The campus visit is a key factor for eliminating schools from your list or making decisions about where and when to apply. It's okay if you can't make it to every school to which you want to apply. You can take extra steps to research an institution thoroughly without visiting, although we encourage a visit whenever possible.


4. Finalize your list and start your applications

Hopefully you now have 7 to 10 schools to which you'll be applying that are appropriately balanced between the 3 categories: 2 challenging schools, 3 just right schools, and 2 no problem schools. And now it's time to organize the application process---figuring out how and when to apply and what each school requires.
